# F45 candidate decision — cross-bridge 3.5% triplet (codata · cmb · nist Be)

**Status: DECLINE.** Triplet collapses to a doublet under consistent unit-framing, and the surviving doublet is not statistically anomalous. Document preserved for negative-result provenance (raw 73 admissibility — failed promotions are first-class evidence).

Origin: `design/hexa_sim/2026-04-26_cross_bridge_correlation_hunt.md` §"Headline finding: 3.5%-fractional-gap triplet".

**The fatal observation**: the F38-doc triplet uses *two different conventions*:
- codata → **absolute** gap (no normalization): 0.036
- cmb → absolute = relative (anchor = 1, so they coincide): 0.035
- Be → **relative to 9 eV** anchor (because absolute would be 0.323 eV, dimensionful): 0.0359

Only by mixing absolute (codata) with relative (Be) does the "triplet" appear. Apply consistent normalization:

- **All-absolute**: codata 0.036, cmb 0.035, Be **0.323 eV** → cmb and codata match, Be diverges by ~10×.
- **All-relative-to-anchor (the F38 stated framing)**: codata **0.000263**, cmb 0.035, Be 0.0359 → codata diverges by ~130×.
- **Bridge-self-emitted gap** (the sentinel each bridge actually claims): codata 0.0263%, cmb 3.5%, Be (not directly emitted) — order-of-magnitude mismatch.

**No single self-consistent convention puts all three in the same band.** The F38 doc itself flags this as Caveat #4 ("F10 framing fragility"); on rigorous review the caveat dominates.

## B. Statistical robustness (random-baseline computation)

For all 16 healthy bridges, 29 dimensionless / scaled-integer values were extracted (full list in computation log; matches doc §Number table). Computing each value's relative-to-nearest-integer-anchor gap (the F38-doc framing):

- 15 of 29 fall in the **0.5%–10% gap band**.
- In the 3.5% ± 0.1pp window (0.034–0.036): exactly **2** values: cmb.n_s=0.0350, nist.Be_ion=0.0359. Width 0.000889.
- Right outside the window: nist.B_ion=0.0373 (boron, also Z<10 first-row). Adding B widens to 0.0023.
- Codata (under consistent rel-to-anchor framing) lives at 0.000263 — 130× away from the cluster.

### Random-uniform baseline (50,000 Monte-Carlo trials)

Drawing 29 i.i.d. uniform gaps on [0, 0.5]:
- P(any doublet within 0.001 envelope, anywhere)  = **0.8148**
- P(any triplet within 0.001 envelope, anywhere) = **0.0397**

Drawing 15 i.i.d. uniform gaps restricted to the [0.005, 0.10] in-band region:
- P(doublet) = **0.9089**, P(triplet) = **0.1216**.

**The observed cmb·Be doublet at 0.000889 envelope is consistent with chance** (~80–90% baseline rate). The doc's claimed "~30× over noise" comes from treating the codata absolute-gap as a third element — i.e. the inflation comes entirely from the framing inconsistency in §A.

### Atomic-shell regularity

First-row (Z=1..6) first-ionization gaps to nearest integer eV:

| element | ion (eV) | nearest int | gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| H | 13.598 | 14 | 2.87% |
| He | 24.587 | 25 | 1.65% |
| Li | 5.392 | 5 | 7.84% |
| **Be** | **9.323** | **9** | **3.59%** |
| **B** | **8.298** | **8** | **3.73%** |
| C | 11.260 | 11 | 2.36% |

Two of six first-row elements (Be, B) sit in the 3.5% band by ordinary atomic-shell structure. The "Be 3.59%" is not a rare cosmic coincidence — it's where Be happens to land between He's and C's noble-gas shells. The doc's Caveat #5 (look-elsewhere) applies in full force.

## C. Decision: **DECLINE F45**

Rationale (any one would be sufficient; all three apply):

1. **Framing is internally inconsistent.** The "triplet" requires comparing codata's absolute gap (0.036) against Be's relative gap (0.0359). Under any single self-consistent convention — all-absolute or all-relative-to-anchor — codata leaves the band by a factor of ~130×. The bridge's own sentinel (`gap_pct=0.0263 %`, rel-to-α) confirms codata's natural gap is 0.026%, not 3.6%.

2. **Doublet survives but is not anomalous.** Removing codata leaves cmb.n_s=0.0350 and nist.Be=0.0359, gap 0.000889. Random-uniform baseline gives ≥80% probability of finding such a doublet anywhere in the bridge value pool by chance. P-value of observed doublet ≈ 0.8 (i.e. ordinary).

3. **Atomic-shell explanation absorbs the Be observation.** Both Be (3.59%) and B (3.73%) sit in the 3.5% band; this is a regularity of how first-row first-ionization energies fall between noble-gas shell anchors, not a cosmology↔atomic resonance. Adding more atomic data (B, plus Z=7..10 for second row) would dilute "Be ≡ n_s" into "first-row atomic ionizations are often 2–8% off integer eV."

The F10 doublet (codata · cmb, both at ~0.036 absolute) remains intact as a sole-witness coincidence — it is **not** strengthened by the proposed F38/F45 extension; the proposed extension actually weakens F10 by exposing its framing fragility. F10 should stand on its own merits (single domain-independent doublet, reasonable strength), and the cross_bridge_correlation_hunt doc should be amended to remove the "30× over noise" claim and the triplet headline.

## D'. What would unlock PROMOTE

A future F45 reviving this idea would need ALL of:

1. **Consistent unit convention** declared up front, with all three values derived under it. Either:
   - All-absolute: would require codata's natural gap to be 0.035 (it is) AND a third value with anchor=1 or known dimensionless natural form (n_s qualifies; Be does not — its absolute gap is 0.323 eV).
   - All-relative: would require a third value where the relative gap (not absolute) is 0.035. Be qualifies under rel-to-9eV; codata does not (rel is 0.026%).
2. **Independence proof for the third element.** The atomic-shell regularity (B 3.73%, Be 3.59%) means Be cannot be promoted to "rare witness" without showing the rest of the periodic table does NOT systematically cluster in 3–4%. A scan of Z=1..18 first-ionization gaps would settle this; if >25% of elements fall in 3–4%, the Be coincidence is explained away.
3. **Statistical power**. Even fixing the above, P(doublet) ≈ 0.8 and P(triplet) ≈ 0.04 under the look-elsewhere correction. To clear paper-grade significance the triplet would need to be tighter than 0.0001 envelope, or have a fourth independent element.

If a future scan finds, say, a fourth dimensionless gap at 0.0350 ± 0.0005 from a domain that is NOT atomic-shell-structured AND NOT cosmological, with consistent unit convention, then F45 could be revisited.
