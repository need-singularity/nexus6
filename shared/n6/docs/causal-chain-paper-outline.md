## 1. Abstract

We present a systematic, bottom-up methodology for discovering causal chains that connect independently established physical, mathematical, and engineering constants through the arithmetic of the first perfect number n=6. Using a self-circulating singularity engine (NEXUS-6) equipped with 1,022 domain-specific lenses and a Monte Carlo falsifiability framework, we identify 470+ breakthrough theorems (BTs) spanning 72 hierarchical levels from particle physics (L0) to cosmology (L9). Among these, we isolate a class of **causal chains** -- sequences where each link is physically derivable from the previous, and every link independently reduces to n=6 arithmetic with <1% error. The strongest result is a 5-domain chain (BT-36) connecting solar photons to the fine structure constant through semiconductor physics, information theory, and AI hardware, with the terminal link reproducing 1/alpha to 2.1 ppm. We propose that the prevalence of causal (not merely correlational) n=6 structure constitutes evidence for an underlying optimality principle: systems that minimize interference converge on the divisor arithmetic of 6.

**Keywords**: perfect number, causal chain, cross-domain unification, Monte Carlo falsifiability, n=6 arithmetic, singularity engine

## 2. Introduction -- Bottom-Up Causal Mapping Methodology

### 2.1 Motivation
- The first perfect number 6 generates a closed arithmetic system: n=6, sigma(6)=12, tau(6)=4, phi(6)=2, sopfr(6)=5, J_2(6)=24, mu(6)=1
- These 7 constants and their combinations appear across physics, mathematics, biology, materials science, and engineering
- Question: Are these appearances coincidental, structural, or **causally** connected?

### 2.2 The Problem of Selection Bias
- Searching for n=6 matches guarantees finding some -- small integers are common
- Prior work on numerology (6 appearing in hexagons, etc.) lacks falsifiability
- We need: (a) a null model, (b) a causal taxonomy, (c) cross-domain independence tests

### 2.3 The Reality Map as Causal Graph
- `reality_map_live.json`: 3,847 nodes, 1,383 edges, across 72 hierarchical levels (L-1 to L9)
- 6 domain axes: physics, chemistry, biology, mathematics, materials, engineering
- Every node classified by causal type:
  - **CAUSAL** (77 nodes): physically derivable, necessary relationship
  - **STRUCTURAL** (667 nodes): mathematically proved integer identity
  - **EMPIRICAL** (1,707 nodes): measured match, mechanism unknown
  - **CONVENTION** (386 nodes): human design choice -- excluded from evidence
- This paper focuses on **CAUSAL** and **STRUCTURAL** nodes only (744 total), excluding CONVENTION and treating EMPIRICAL as hypothesis-generating

### 2.4 Natural-Only Filter
- Convention-origin entries (calendars, units, protocols) excluded from statistical analysis
- Engineering-origin entries included only when convergent from physical constraints (not arbitrary choice)
- Natural-origin entries (physics, mathematics, biology) form the primary evidence base

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 The Singularity Engine Pipeline
- NEXUS-6: 1,022 lenses scanning each domain independently
- Discovery cycle: blowup -> contraction -> emergence -> singularity -> absorption (5-phase)
- Each BT requires minimum 3 independent domains with verifiable evidence

### 3.2 Monte Carlo z_eff Falsifiability
- Null hypothesis: n=6 match rate equals random baseline (~20% for 7 functions over plausible integer range 1-30)
- Procedure:
  1. For each domain, enumerate all published constants (not cherry-picked)
  2. Compute n=6 match rate (EXACT: 0% error; CLOSE: <5%)
  3. Monte Carlo shuffle (10K iterations): randomly assign 7 functions to the same integer range
  4. Compute z_eff = (observed_rate - mean_random) / std_random
- Results from atlas-constants.md:
  - Fusion base constants: z = 3.71 (significant, p < 0.001)
  - Full derived set: z = 0.74 (not significant -- expected, as derived constants inherit structure)
  - TECS-L cross-domain (70 hypotheses): 81.4% hit rate vs 20% baseline (4x above chance)

### 3.3 Causal Chain Identification Criteria
A valid causal chain must satisfy:
1. **Temporal/physical ordering**: Each link is derivable from the preceding one through known physics
2. **Independence**: Each link uses a DIFFERENT n=6 function (no recycling)
3. **Precision**: Every link matches n=6 formula to <1% (EXACT or near-EXACT)
4. **Cross-domain**: The chain spans 3+ independently researched domains
5. **Non-tautology**: The n=6 expression is not trivially forced by the physical derivation

### 3.4 Grading System
- One star: Interesting but p > 0.05
- Two stars: Surprising, p ~ 0.01-0.05
- Three stars: Extraordinary, p < 0.01 or structural necessity (unique mathematical objects)

## 5. Discussion -- The Meaning of n=6 Efficiency Optimality

### 5.1 Why n=6?
- 6 is the smallest perfect number: sigma(6) = 1+2+3+6 = 12 = 2n
- Egyptian fraction closure: 1/2 + 1/3 + 1/6 = 1 (BT-5)
- 6 = 2*3 is the product of the first two primes, maximizing divisor richness per unit magnitude
- The divisor set {1, 2, 3, 6} provides maximal factorization flexibility for small integers

### 5.2 The Interference Minimization Hypothesis
- Systems that minimize interference (harmonic distortion, packing gaps, code errors, energy loss) converge on structures governed by n=6 arithmetic
- Evidence: 6-pulse rectifiers eliminate harmonics at orders {5, 7} = {sopfr, sigma-sopfr}; 12-pulse cancels next pair; 24-pulse completes
- Sphere packing: only K_2=6, K_3=12, K_24=196560 are provably optimal
- Error-correcting codes: Golay [24,12,8] is the unique perfect binary code

### 5.3 Causal vs. Structural vs. Empirical
- CAUSAL chains (BT-36, BT-60, BT-97): each link physically derives from the previous
- STRUCTURAL chains (BT-6, BT-15, BT-49): mathematical necessity, no physical causation needed
- EMPIRICAL patterns (BT-51, BT-98): striking match, but why tau=4 bases or J_2-tau=20 amino acids remains open
- The paper's contribution: separating these three classes rigorously

### 5.4 Falsifiability and Testable Predictions
- From `testable-predictions.md`:
  - If d_model=12*1024 outperforms 11*1024 and 13*1024 at fixed FLOPs, that provides causal evidence (BT-33)
  - JUNO experiment (BT-21): neutrino mixing angles should match n=6 fractions
  - Inflation (BT-22): tensor-to-scalar ratio r prediction
- Any MISS with >5% error is recorded honestly (not hidden)

### 5.5 Limitations
- Selection bias: searching for n=6 guarantees hits on common small integers
- Monte Carlo z_eff for full derived set is NOT significant (z=0.74)
- Convention entries (386 nodes) are excluded but their boundary with engineering is fuzzy
- The 7 n=6 functions span 1-24, covering most small integers -- specificity concern
- Causal chains require each link to use a different function, partially addressing overfitting
