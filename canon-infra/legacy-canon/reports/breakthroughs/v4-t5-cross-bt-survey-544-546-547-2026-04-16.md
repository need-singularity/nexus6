## §3 BT-544 (Navier-Stokes) — domain not approached by this architecture

### 3.1 Clay statement (Fefferman 2000)

Given $f \in C^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3; \mathbb{R}^3)$ rapidly decaying + $\nabla \cdot u_0 = 0$,
- **Existence + regularity**: a smooth solution $(u, p)$ to NS on $\mathbb{R}^3 \times [0, \infty)$ exists, or
- **Breakdown**: finite-time blowup occurs from smooth initial data

**Proving either side** resolves the Clay problem.

### 3.2 Current progress (2026-04)

**Main results related to 3D Navier-Stokes**:

| Year | Author | Result | Impact |
|------|------|------|------|
| 1934 | Leray | existence of weak solution | foundational |
| 1951 | Hopf | finite-energy weak solution, global | foundational |
| 2002 | Koch-Tataru | $BMO^{-1}$ local well-posedness | fundamental |
| 2014 | Buckmaster-Vicol | convex integration weak solutions (Onsager type) | conceptual |
| 2021 | Elgindi | $C^{1,\alpha}$ Euler singularity (not NS) | 3D blowup hope |
| 2023 | Tao | barrier: supercriticality + averaged equations (conditional blowup) | **core modern** |
| 2025+ | various | follow-ups to the Hou-Luo 2014 exact blowup scenario | ongoing |

### 3.3 The modern barrier — supercriticality

**Tao 2009 (blog) + 2023 refinement** key observations:
- NS has **supercritical** scaling invariance: $u(x,t) \to \lambda u(\lambda x, \lambda^2 t)$, with mass $\to \lambda^{-1/2}$
- The small-solution regime is tractable, but for large data **every energy-like quantity** is supercritical
- Therefore **"soft" arguments** cannot prevent blowup → one must **use the nonlinear structure directly**

**Tao 2023 "conditional blowup"**:
- Blowup can be constructed for specific "averaged equations"
- Separation from genuine NS remains open
- A genuine NS blowup proof or a regularity proof → resolves Clay

### 3.4 Re-examining this project's BT-544 entry "n=6 tensor structure" claim

The internal BT-544 entry (breakthrough-theorems.md:19920) claims **"independent components of a 3D (n/φ) symmetric tensor = 6"**:
- A 3×3 symmetric tensor → 6 independent components = n
- This is a **mathematical fact** but has **no direct relationship** to NS solution regularity/existence
- Symmetric stress tensors are common to all continuum mechanics (not unique to NS)

**Honest reassessment**: This project's BT-544 "n=6 tensor" connection is **mathematically correct but meaningless for the Clay problem** — the Clay problem requires a regularity proof, and the tensor count is a long-known constant.

**Conclusion**: There is **no essential connection** between NS regularity/blowup and n=6 structure. NS is centered on **PDE analysis + harmonic analysis**.
