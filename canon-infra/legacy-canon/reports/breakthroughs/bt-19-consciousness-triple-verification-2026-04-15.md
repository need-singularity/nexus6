## 1. Definitional Consistency Check (§1)

### 1.1 IIT Phi definition (Barrett-Tononi context)

Search **failed to confirm** the existence of "Barrett & Tononi Phys Rev E 2018". Related Barrett-family Phi papers:

- **Barrett & Seth (2011) PLoS CB 7(1):e1001052** — Phi_E (empirical Phi for Gaussian time-series) definition
- **Barrett & Mediano (2019) J Conscious Stud 26(1-2):11-20** — Phi is not well-defined on general physical systems **(refuting paper)**
- **Mediano, Seth, Barrett (2019) Entropy 21:17** — comparison of candidate Phi measures; reports "no two measures agree across all analyses"

**Core problem**: Phi itself is not a single latent variable. Balduzzi-Tononi Phi^DM, Barrett-Seth Phi_E, Oizumi Phi*, and Tononi IIT 3.0 Phi_MIP produce different numbers, sharing only the name "Phi".

### 1.2 GWT ignition definition (Dehaene context)

Reviewing Dehaene-Changeux-Naccache 2011 Neuron and Mashour-Roelfsema-Changeux-Dehaene 2020 Neuron review:

- ignition is a **nonlinear all-or-none** phenomenon — step-function threshold (Del Cul 2007, Sergent 2005)
- **not parametrized by a power-law exponent alpha**
- Measured signals: P300 (P3b) latency ~300 ms, fronto-parietal BOLD amplification, long-range gamma synchrony

**The "alpha = 3/4" number is nowhere in Dehaene 2011/2014.** The parent P7-1 document's citations of Cowan 2001 (bottleneck = 4) and Dehaene 2005 (~12 ROI) are real, but their reinterpretation as "scaling exponents" is post-hoc processing by this project.

### 1.3 Independence judgement

- Conceptual independence: Phi (local causation) vs ignition (global broadcasting) — **YES**
- Empirical independence: Casali 2013 PCI (IIT-derived) vs GNW fMRI ignition — high correlation across same-subject states (Sarasso 2015 propofol/xenon/ketamine PCI 0.12-0.31 unconscious vs 0.44-0.67 awake; GNW ignition also disappears in the same contrast)
- **Conclusion**: the two measures capture the **same latent (level of consciousness)** through different proxies, so even if the alpha product = 1 is true, it is **not a non-trivial finding**
