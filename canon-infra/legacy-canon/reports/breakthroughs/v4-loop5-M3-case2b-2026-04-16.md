```lean
theorem theorem_B_odd_pq {p q : ℕ} (hp : p.Prime) (hq : q.Prime)
    (hp3 : p ≥ 3) (hq3 : q ≥ 3) (hpq : p ≠ q) :
    σ 1 (p * q) * Nat.totient (p * q) ≠ (p * q) * (Nat.divisors (p * q)).card
```

**Auxiliary lemmas**:
- `coprime_odd_distinct_primes`: p, q distinct primes → gcd(p, q) = 1
- `sigma_one_pq`: σ(pq) = (p+1)(q+1) for coprime primes
- `totient_pq`: φ(pq) = (p-1)(q-1) for coprime primes
- `tau_pq`: τ(pq) = 4 for coprime primes

## §3 Core demonstration (concise)

Assume the equality $\sigma(pq) \cdot \varphi(pq) = pq \cdot \tau(pq)$ and derive a contradiction:
$$(p+1)(q+1) \cdot (p-1)(q-1) = 4pq$$

**Observations**:
- $p \geq 3 \Rightarrow p - 1 \geq 2$; likewise $q - 1 \geq 2$
- $(p-1)(q-1) \geq 4$
- $(p+1)(q+1) = pq + p + q + 1 > pq$

**Combined**:
$$(p+1)(q+1) \cdot (p-1)(q-1) \geq (p+1)(q+1) \cdot 4 > 4pq$$

Contradiction.

**Lean4 implementation**:
```lean
  intro h
  have hpm1_qm1 : (p - 1) * (q - 1) ≥ 4 := ...
  have hpp1_qp1 : (p + 1) * (q + 1) > p * q := ...
  have step1 : (p + 1) * (q + 1) * ((p - 1) * (q - 1)) ≥ (p + 1) * (q + 1) * 4 :=
    Nat.mul_le_mul_left _ hpm1_qm1
  have step2 : (p + 1) * (q + 1) * 4 > p * q * 4 := ...
  omega  -- combining h, step1, step2 → False
```

### 6.1 Case 3 difficulty

$n = p^a$, $a \geq 2$:
- $\sigma(p^a) = (p^{a+1} - 1)/(p - 1)$
- $\varphi(p^a) = p^{a-1}(p - 1)$
- $\tau(p^a) = a + 1$

Equality: $(p^{a+1} - 1) \cdot p^{a-1} = p^a \cdot (a + 1)$
$p^{2a} - p^{a-1} = p^a (a+1)$
$p^a (p^a - a - 1) = p^{a-1}$
$p \cdot (p^a - a - 1) = 1$

Since $p \geq 2$, $p \cdot (\ldots) = 1$ would require $p = 1$ → contradiction. Hence no equality.

(Note: mind the sign of $p^a - a - 1$ — for $a \geq 2$, $p \geq 2$: $p^a \geq 4$, $a + 1 \leq ?$. At $p = 2, a = 2$: $p^a - a - 1 = 4 - 3 = 1 > 0$. In general $p^a \geq a + 2$ for $p \geq 2, a \geq 1$, so $p^a - a - 1 \geq 1$, never zero.)

Lean4 implementation: v4 follow-on.
