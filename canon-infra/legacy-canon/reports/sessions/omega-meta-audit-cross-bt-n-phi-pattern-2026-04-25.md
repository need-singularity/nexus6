## §1 Hypothesis statement

- **H_real**: n/φ ≈ 3 is a recurring *structural* axis across
  BT-544 / 545 / 546 because n=6 forces the same arithmetic
  decomposition `(n=6, φ(6)=2, n/φ=3, τ(6)=4, σ(6)=12, sopfr(6)=5)`,
  and BT-attack frames import classical results indexed at dim 3
  whose role *predates* the n=6 framing. Under H_real, the integer 3
  does mathematical work in each BT (dimension of an irreducible
  object, codimension of an obstruction, count of a discrete
  invariant) and the cross-BT recurrence is a *true* coincidence
  forced by complex/real geometry's preference for low odd dimension.

- **H_numerology**: BT-544 / 545 / 546 *each* generated their own
  decomposition independently; the apparent shared n/φ=3 pattern is
  post-hoc framing or selection bias from analysts who knew n=6
  played a special role and labelled `3` as `n/φ` only after the
  underlying classical result was imported.

- **H_confounded**: the n=6-architecture project's own probe-selection
  pipeline systematically reaches for classical results that *happen*
  to be indexed at dimension 3 (Calabi-Yau-3, complex 3-fold,
  rank-3 lattice, three-phase decomposition); the recurrence then
  reflects analyst selection along a **dimension axis** that is
  perfectly collinear with the **n/φ=3 axis** at the current sample
  size, which is the same kind of confounding diagnosed in
  `omega-meta-audit-discriminator-type-bias-2026-04-25.md`.

**Distinguishing test**: do the n/φ=3 instances *do mathematical
work* — i.e. do they appear as
- (a) dimensions of irreducible representations / cohomology spaces,
- (b) indices of exceptional subgroups / codimensions of strata, or
- (c) counts of generators / components / discrete invariants —
or do they appear only as *labels* attached to results that already
carry the integer 3 from outside the n=6 framing?
