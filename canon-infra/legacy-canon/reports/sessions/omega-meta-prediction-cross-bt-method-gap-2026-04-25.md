## §1 Method-gap pattern definition (from BT-542 + BT-544 R1)

### §1.1 The pattern

From the two confirmed parent reports, the method-gap pattern has the
following shape:

> Each conjecture C lives in a **region** R of statement-space. Two
> complementary technique families F_A (construction-side) and F_B
> (rigidity-side) each reach **part** of R. The *method gap* G is the
> region of R unreached by F_A ∪ F_B. The conjecture sits in G.
> Closing C requires a technique in G — either bridging F_A and F_B,
> or introducing a third family F_C that covers G directly.

The pattern is **descriptive**: it maps why C is hard, not how to
close C.

### §1.2 Confirmed instances

**BT-544 R1 (Onsager–Hölder strip)**:

- F_A = convex integration (De Lellis–Székelyhidi → Isett 2018 on
  Euler; BV 2019, BCV 2021, Cheskidov–Luo 2022 on NS). Reaches α_BV
  ≪ 1/3 on the construction side.
- F_B = energy-conservation rigidity (Constantin–E–Titi 1994).
  Requires α > 1/3 on the rigidity side.
- G = the strip α ∈ (α_BV, 1/3). C3 places α*_NS in G strictly.
- Source: `omega-exec-bt544-r1-lemma1-level4-cet-2026-04-25.md` §6,
  §8.4.

**BT-542 P=NP (4-barrier intersection)**:

- F_A = specific-algorithm side (P-side construction; algorithms
  resolving NP-complete problems in P-time would close one direction).
- F_B = lower-bound side (Razborov–Rudich 1994/1997 STOC/JCSS, AW
  2008 ToC, BGS 1975 SIAM JC, Mulmuley 2001+ / Ikenmeyer–Panova
  2017 — each barrier theorem retires a technique class).
- G = {non-relativizing ∩ non-natural ∩ non-algebrizing ∩
  non-GCT-occurrence-class} ∩ {techniques reaching NP rather than
  NEXP/NQP/MCSP-variants}.
- Williams 2011 (NEXP ⊄ ACC^0) / Williams 2018 (NQP ⊄ ACC^0) and
  Hirahara 2018 FOCS / 2022 FOCS populate G but reach only NEXP /
  NQP / partial-MCSP, not the NP target.
- Source: `omega-exec-bt542-4barrier-progressive-deepening-2026-04-
  25.md` §6, §7.2.

### §1.3 Pattern elements

The two confirmed instances share five descriptive elements:

1. **Two complementary families** F_A, F_B exist in published
   literature.
2. **Each family has bounded reach** — not unlimited, with the
   bounds documented by published theorems.
3. **The reach-complements are disjoint** in some natural parameter
   (Hölder exponent for BT-544; technique-class predicate for
   BT-542).
4. **The conjecture lives in the gap** between the reaches.
5. **No published technique in the gap closes the conjecture** —
   either the gap is empty in current literature, or known
   gap-populating techniques (Williams, Hirahara) reach only
   *adjacent* targets.

Predictions for BT-543/545/546 (§2–§4) verify that all five elements
are present in the published literature on each BT.

## §2 BT-543 Yang-Mills (mass gap) prediction

### §2.1 Statement

**Predicted method gap** (low confidence, non-binding):

> BT-543's method gap = **lattice numerics ↔ continuum rigor**.
> Lattice gauge theory simulations (Wilson 1974) approximate the
> mass gap empirically; rigorous lower bounds require continuum
> existence (Wightman / Osterwalder–Schrader axioms) which is open
> in 4D non-abelian gauge theories.

### §2.2 Family A: lattice gauge theory (construction-side numerics)

**Citation**: K. Wilson, "Confinement of quarks", *Phys. Rev. D* 10
(1974), 2445–2459 (lattice formulation). FLAG (Flavor Lattice
Averaging Group) 2024 review for current numerical state.

Lattice gauge theory discretizes 4D Euclidean spacetime on a finite
hypercubic lattice and computes gauge-invariant correlators
numerically. Key features:

- **Finite-volume + finite-spacing** controlled approximation: each
  correlator is computed at fixed (a, L), and continuum + infinite-
  volume extrapolations are taken numerically, not rigorously.
- **Mass-gap measurement**: correlators of glueball operators decay
  exponentially in Euclidean time with rate m_0++; FLAG 2024
  reports m_0++ ≈ 1.6 GeV with B^E confinement exponent ≈ 4.0 ± 0.1
  for SU(3) pure gauge across [10, 20, 30, 40, 48] scale sweep
  (`omega-cycle-bt543-ym-2026-04-25.md` §1).
- **Reach**: lattice produces *empirical evidence* of a positive
  mass gap m_0++ > 0 in 4D SU(N) pure gauge, consistent with the
  Clay statement.
- **Bound**: lattice cannot produce a *rigorous proof* of m_0++ > 0
  in the continuum; the continuum limit (a → 0, L → ∞) is taken
  numerically. The Symanzik improvement program reduces O(a) errors
  but does not provide a rigorous existence theorem.

### §2.3 Family B: continuum axiomatic field theory (rigidity-side)

**Citation**: A. Wightman, "Quantum field theory in terms of vacuum
expectation values", *Phys. Rev.* 101 (1956), 860–866 (Wightman
axioms). K. Osterwalder, R. Schrader, "Axioms for Euclidean Green's
functions", *Comm. Math. Phys.* 31 (1973), 83–112 / *Comm. Math.
Phys.* 42 (1975), 281–305 (OS axioms; Wick rotation reconstruction).

Continuum-axiomatic Yang–Mills mass-gap proof requires:

- **Constructive existence**: build a Wightman / OS QFT satisfying
  the Yang–Mills Lagrangian, in 4D Minkowski / Euclidean spacetime.
- **Mass-gap inference**: prove the spectrum of the Hamiltonian (or
  the OS Euclidean correlator decay rate) has a strictly positive
  lower bound in the gauge-invariant sector.
- **Reach**: rigorous continuum constructions exist for **lower
  dimensions** — Glimm–Jaffe 1968+ (φ^4 in 2D, 3D), Magnen–Sénéor
  for various boson models. For **2D** Yang–Mills, Driver / Sengupta
  / Lévy provide constructions but the 2D case is dimensionally
  trivial for confinement.
- **Bound**: no rigorous Wightman / OS reconstruction of 4D
  non-abelian Yang–Mills exists. The constructive program in 4D
  has not closed even existence (let alone mass gap).

### §2.4 The gap

Both families reach part of the answer-space:

- **Family A** reaches "lattice evidence m_0++ ≈ 1.6 GeV" but stops
  short of rigorous continuum existence.
- **Family B** reaches "rigorous continuum existence in 2D /
  weakly-coupled bosonic models" but stops short of 4D non-abelian
  Yang–Mills.

The gap G_543 is the bridge:

> *G_543 = {a rigorous proof that the lattice continuum limit
> exists for 4D SU(N) pure gauge AND has a positive mass gap}*.

The gap is non-empty as a conjectured region (the Clay statement
itself asserts G_543 is non-empty in the rigorously-true sense).
Current techniques in G_543: **none**. The Jaffe–Witten Clay
problem statement explicitly identifies this bridge as the open
problem.

### §2.5 Pattern element coverage

| element | present? | source |
|---------|----------|--------|
| 1. two complementary families | YES | F_A lattice (Wilson 1974+), F_B axiomatic (Wightman/OS 1956–1975) |
| 2. each family has bounded reach | YES | F_A: numerical, no continuum-existence theorem; F_B: 2D / weakly-coupled, no 4D-non-abelian theorem |
| 3. reach-complements disjoint | YES | F_A operates at (a > 0, L < ∞); F_B requires (a = 0, L = ∞) with full QFT axioms |
| 4. conjecture lives in gap | YES | Clay statement = positive 4D continuum mass gap, exactly the G_543 bridge |
| 5. no current technique closes | YES | constructive QFT in 4D non-abelian gauge has been open since Yang–Mills 1954, Jaffe–Witten Clay statement 2000 |

All five elements present.

### §2.6 Caveats

- The FLAG 2024 number m_0++ ≈ 1.6 GeV is for **SU(3) pure gauge**;
  the Clay statement covers SU(N) for general N. The gap is the
  same shape, but different numerics.
- "Lattice continuum limit" is itself a multi-step program (Symanzik
  improvement, asymptotic freedom β-function, perturbative–
  nonperturbative matching); the gap may decompose into sub-gaps,
  analogous to BT-544 R1's L1–L4 progressive deepening.
- 2D Yang–Mills constructions (Driver, Sengupta, Lévy) are real and
  rigorous, but 2D is *dimensionally exceptional* (no asymptotic
  freedom, no propagating gluons); they do not extrapolate to 4D.

## §3 BT-545 Hodge prediction

### §3.1 Statement

**Predicted method gap** (low confidence, non-binding):

> BT-545's method gap = **explicit algebraic cycles ↔ general
> rational Hodge classes**. Explicit constructions cover specific
> surfaces / abelian varieties / CY3 cases; general rationality
> arguments (over ℚ, not ℤ) remain open at the level of arbitrary
> smooth projective varieties.

### §3.2 Family A: explicit algebraic cycles (construction-side)

**Citations** (by author/year/venue level only):

- S. Lefschetz, "L'Analysis Situs et la Géométrie Algébrique"
  (1924) — (1,1)-lemma, full coverage of (1,1) cohomology.
- P. Deligne, "Hodge cycles on abelian varieties" (in *Hodge
  Cycles, Motives, and Shimura Varieties*, LNM 900, 1982; abelian-
  variety k=1 / Weil-class results inherited from earlier work).
- C. Voisin, work on K3 surfaces and integral / rational Hodge
  classes (Voisin 2002–2007 monograph series).
- S. Bloch, "Lectures on algebraic cycles" (Duke / Cambridge 1980/
  2010) — Bloch's conjecture for surfaces of geometric genus 0.
- Mostaed (arXiv 2603.20268, 2026) — conditional abelian sixfold
  via McMullen-curve parametrization.

Reach (per `omega-cycle-bt545-hodge-2026-04-25.md` §1.1):

- **τ_1**: Lefschetz (1,1)-lemma — full (1,1) coverage.
- **τ_2**: Deligne k=1 abelian — single-power abelian.
- **τ_3**: Weil Hodge classes under CM-form positivity.
- **τ_4**: Mostaed conditional abelian sixfold.
- **Open locus**: general CY3, general abelian 6-fold without CM,
  general projective varieties of dim ≥ 4.

### §3.3 Family B: integral-vs-rational rigidity (rigidity-side)

**Citations**:

- M. Atiyah, F. Hirzebruch, "Analytische Zyklen auf komplex-
  algebraischen Mannigfaltigkeiten", *Topology* 1 (1961), 25–45 —
  counterexamples to **integral** Hodge conjecture (some integral
  Hodge classes are not algebraic).
- J. Kollár, "Trento examples" (in *Classification of irregular
  varieties*, 1992) — additional integral Hodge counterexamples.
- C. Voisin, "On integral Hodge classes on uniruled or
  Calabi–Yau threefolds", in various venues mid-2000s — refined
  integral counterexamples.

Reach: the **integral** version of the Hodge conjecture is
**known false** — the rigidity side definitively rules it out, on
explicit projective varieties.

Bound: the **rational** version (Hodge classes ⊗ ℚ are ℚ-linear
combinations of algebraic-cycle classes) remains open. Atiyah–
Hirzebruch / Kollár counterexamples vanish under tensoring with ℚ
(they are torsion phenomena). Therefore Family B's rigidity does
**not** apply to the rational version; it only retires the
integral version.

### §3.4 The gap

- **Family A** reaches specific cases (τ_1..τ_4 above) but not
  general projective varieties.
- **Family B** retires the integral version but is **silent** on
  the rational version.

The gap G_545:

> *G_545 = {a general construction or rigidity argument applicable
> to **rational** Hodge classes on **general** smooth projective
> varieties (dim ≥ 4, non-CM, non-Calabi–Yau-threefold)}*.

Current techniques in G_545: **none generally**. The conjecture
sits in G_545 by hypothesis.

### §3.5 Pattern element coverage

| element | present? | source |
|---------|----------|--------|
| 1. two complementary families | YES | F_A explicit cycles (Lefschetz / Deligne / Voisin K3 / Bloch / Mostaed); F_B integral-Hodge counterexamples (Atiyah–Hirzebruch 1961 / Kollár 1992 / Voisin) |
| 2. each family has bounded reach | YES | F_A: τ_1..τ_4 partial cases; F_B: integral version only, not rational |
| 3. reach-complements disjoint | YES | F_A handles specific varieties; F_B handles integral failures, not rational success |
| 4. conjecture lives in gap | YES | Hodge conjecture (rational version) = the open region between F_A's cases and F_B's silence |
| 5. no current technique closes | YES | rational Hodge open at general dimension ≥ 4 since 1950 |

All five elements present.

### §3.6 Caveats

- The pattern's "construction vs rigidity" labels are looser for
  BT-545 than for BT-544. Family A is genuinely construction-side
  (build the algebraic cycle); Family B is *partial-rigidity* (rule
  out integral) but does not constrain the rational target. The
  asymmetry is structural to the conjecture's integral-vs-rational
  split.
- Voevodsky motivic / Beilinson–Lichtenbaum framework (per
  `omega-cycle-bt545-hodge-2026-04-25.md` §1.4 P2 probe) introduces
  a *third* family (motivic). The 2-family pattern is a
  simplification; a 3-family version would refine §3.4's gap
  characterization.
- The Mostaed 2026 conditional result (τ_4) tightens F_A's reach
  but does not close G_545.

## §4 BT-546 BSD prediction

### §4.1 Statement

**Predicted method gap** (low confidence, non-binding):

> BT-546's method gap = **Heegner-style explicit constructions
> (rank ≤ 1) ↔ rank ≥ 2 general structure**. Heegner-point
> constructions exhaust analytic / algebraic rank 0 and 1 cases;
> rank ≥ 2 requires a fundamentally different construction or
> structural argument, currently unavailable.

### §4.2 Family A: Heegner / modular constructions (construction-side)

**Citations**:

- B. Gross, D. Zagier, "Heegner points and derivatives of L-series",
  *Invent. Math.* 84 (1986), 225–320 — Heegner point + derivative
  of L-function relation (rank-1 analytic case).
- V. Kolyvagin, "Finiteness of E(ℚ) and Sha(E,ℚ) for a subclass of
  Weil curves", *Izv. Akad. Nauk SSSR* 52 (1988) — finiteness of
  Sha for analytic rank ≤ 1.
- A. Wiles, "Modular elliptic curves and Fermat's last theorem",
  *Annals* 142 (1995), 443–551 (with Taylor–Wiles) — modularity
  of semistable elliptic curves over ℚ; combined with Breuil–
  Conrad–Diamond–Taylor 2001 for full modularity.
- J. Coates, A. Wiles, "On the conjecture of Birch and Swinnerton-
  Dyer", *Invent. Math.* 39 (1977), 223–251 — analytic rank 0 case
  for CM curves.

Reach (per `omega-cycle-bt546-bsd-2026-04-25.md` §1):

- Analytic rank 0: Coates–Wiles 1977 (CM); Kolyvagin / Gross–Zagier
  for general modular elliptic curves with analytic rank 0
  (combined with Kolyvagin's Euler systems).
- Analytic rank 1: Gross–Zagier 1986 + Kolyvagin 1988 — full BSD
  for analytic rank ≤ 1 modular elliptic curves over ℚ.
- Modularity: Wiles 1995 + BCDT 2001 — every elliptic curve over ℚ
  is modular (so the analytic continuation exists, allowing the
  rank-0/1 results to apply universally).

Bound: rank ≥ 2 is **out of reach** of Heegner-point constructions.
The Heegner-point construction produces a single rational point
(modulo torsion); higher-rank cases require multiple independent
points, and no general systematic construction exists. The Cremona
332k empirical sample reaches rank 3 at only 0.04% (130 of 332,366),
giving no inferential leverage for rank ≥ 2.

### §4.3 Family B: rank ≥ 2 general structure (rigidity-side)

This side is *under-populated*. Available techniques include:

- Iwasawa main conjecture (Skinner–Urban 2014, *Invent. Math.* —
  cyclotomic IMC for GL_2 modular forms; relates p-adic L-functions
  to Selmer groups). Provides one half of BSD's p-adic refinement
  but does not directly resolve rank ≥ 2.
- BSD parity conjecture (Dokchitser brothers, 2008–2013) — rank
  parity matches root number, *conditional* on Sha[p] / equivalent
  hypotheses.
- Euler-systems beyond Heegner: Kato's Euler system (1998+) for
  modular forms gives one direction (rank ≤ analytic rank); reverse
  direction (rank ≥ 2 algebraic implies rank ≥ 2 analytic) open.

Reach: produces *partial* control over Sha and ranks under
hypotheses, but does **not** unconditionally resolve rank ≥ 2.

### §4.4 The gap

- **Family A** exhausts analytic rank ≤ 1 unconditionally (modular
  curves over ℚ).
- **Family B** is partially populated (Iwasawa, parity, Kato Euler
  systems) but does not unconditionally resolve rank ≥ 2.

The gap G_546:

> *G_546 = {a construction or structural argument that produces or
> certifies r ≥ 2 independent rational points (algebraic rank ≥ 2)
> when ord_{s=1} L(E, s) ≥ 2 (analytic rank ≥ 2), unconditionally
> over ℚ}*.

Current techniques in G_546: **none unconditionally**. The
conjecture sits in G_546 for all elliptic curves with analytic
rank ≥ 2.

| 2. each family has bounded reach | YES | F_A: rank ≤ 1; F_B: partial / conditional |

### §4.6 Caveats

- F_B is less *theoretically rigid* than BT-544's CET 1994 or
  BT-542's barrier theorems. It is a *cluster* of partial results
  rather than a sharp theorem of the form "technique X cannot
  resolve rank ≥ 2". The 2-family structure is therefore weaker
  on the rigidity side for BT-546.
- The (A3) BKLPR independence hypothesis (`omega-cycle-bt546-bsd
  -2026-04-25.md` §1.2) is itself a Family-B-adjacent assumption;
  it is unproved and constrains the average behavior, not
  individual rank ≥ 2 curves.
- Kim's non-abelian Chabauty program is an emerging F_C-candidate
  third family that may eventually populate G_546 partially. As
  of 2026 it has not closed rank ≥ 2 BSD.

## §7 Predictive value vs operational value

### §7.1 Predictive value

The pattern is **predictive** in the sense that, given any BT's
audit material, one can identify Families A and B from the
literature and characterize the gap G. This:

- locates the BT's difficulty (the gap is where the BT lives);
- explains why naive technique-application fails (each family hits
  its bound and stops);
- predicts where future work *could* fit (in G or bridging F_A
  ↔ F_B);
- registers falsifiers: if a published technique closes G, the
  pattern's "no current technique in gap" element is updated; if
  no Families A/B are identifiable, the pattern doesn't apply.

This predictive value is **non-trivial** but **non-claim**. It does
not produce a P=NP technique, an NS Hölder bound, a rigorous
mass-gap proof, an algebraic cycle for a general Hodge class, or
a Heegner-class construction at rank ≥ 2.

### §7.2 Operational value: zero

Knowing the method gap **does not close the gap**. Specifically:

- For BT-543, identifying the bridge "lattice ↔ continuum" does not
  produce the bridge.
- For BT-544 R1, identifying the strip (α_BV, 1/3) does not produce
  α*_NS (this is exactly L4's negative finding in the parent
  report).
- For BT-545, identifying "explicit cycles ↔ general rational" does
  not produce a general algebraic cycle for an arbitrary Hodge
  class.
- For BT-546, identifying "rank ≤ 1 ↔ rank ≥ 2" does not produce a
  rank-2 construction.
- For BT-542, identifying the 4-barrier intersection does not
  produce a P=NP-resolving technique (Williams/Hirahara confirm
  the intersection is non-empty, but do not close P vs NP at the
  NP target).

The pattern's operational value is **zero** by design: it is a
descriptive lens, not a proof technique.

### §7.3 What the pattern *does* enable

Within research-only / methodology scope:

- Cross-BT comparison: the §5.1 table allows one to compare BT-X's
  difficulty with BT-Y's difficulty at the level of "which
  techniques are missing".
- Falsifier registration: the "5 elements present" check provides a
  template for auditing future open problems' method-gap structure.
- Honest mapping: per `omega-meta-synthesis-progressive-deepening
  -pattern-2026-04-25.md` §3 portable statement, the pattern
  provides a *map of why the problem is hard*, distinct from a
  *technique to solve the problem*. This distinction is itself
  valuable for triage / portfolio selection (which BTs to attack
  with which techniques).

These uses are **methodological** and stay within the scope
disclaimer (§0).

  (per parent report's §8.2 cross-time-scale finding) but the
  *agent of refinement* differs (single author vs distributed
  community).
- The **publication grade** of Family A / Family B differs across
  BTs. BT-544 R1's Family A is post-2014 convex-integration papers
  in *Annals* / *Invent.* / *JEMS*; BT-542's barriers are SIAM JC
  / JCSS / ToC. BT-543's lattice numerics are *Phys. Rev.*

- The pattern's **own falsifiability** is constrained: a future
  technique closing any of G_543, G_545, G_546 (or G_544, G_542)
  would not falsify the pattern itself — it would merely move the
  BT out of the "in-gap, unclosed" state into "gap closed by F_C
  or via F_A↔F_B bridge". The pattern is primarily descriptive of
  the *current state*, not a *prediction about future
  closures*.
