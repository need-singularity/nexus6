## sec 9 -- Methodological lessons (for future sessions)

These are observed practices, not new rules. They are worth importing
into future sessions:

1. **Per-BT 4-axis structural audit before any proof attempt.** All 7
   BTs received the same (ladder / saturation / chain / closure) audit
   shape before any molt validation was attempted. This produced a
   uniform substrate for cross-BT comparison.

2. **Backward-chain synthesis after parallel audits.** `omega-cycle-
   backtrace-strategy` consumes 7 root files; this is cheaper than
   forward-search and yields ROI-ranked next probes.

3. **Meta-falsifier (F-MOLT-A / F-MOLT-D) gates progress.** The
   framework explicitly does not fire on a single FAIL; only on
   catalogue-saturation. This prevented over-rotation when BT-544 Q1
   FAILed cheaply.

4. **Three-pass amendment loop.** v1 honesty / v2 confounded / v3
   cross-cell amendments all add blocks without retracting prior
   verdicts. Verdicts are append-only; the audit trail is preserved.

5. **Cross-repo verification before deploy.** The honesty triad was
   audited n6-natively (3 reports), nexus-natively (1 nexus-native
   re-audit), and ported via design step (porting audit) before any
   deployment file. 6-of-6 KEEP_AS_IS was required.

6. **Catalogue-extension is allowed, generation-pipeline bias is named.**
   Hodge and BSD molt-validations explicitly extend the L9 catalogue;
   the meta-audit on l9-generation-pipeline names the bias that motivated
   the extension.

7. **No-fabrication preserved by INCONCLUSIVE / UNKNOWN markers.** When
   a verdict is uncertain it is marked, not guessed. EXT-tier is `0/0
   terminal` rather than fabricated.
