## 5. Three main critique-section weaknesses (user-requested items)

### 5.1 Weakness 1 — doubt on true independence of the 3 sigma*phi=n*tau demonstrations (SS 11.1)

**Issue**: Demonstrations 1 (algebraic) / 2 (analytic) / 3 (constructive) all use the **multiplicativity** of $\sigma, \phi, \tau$ as a common lemma. Independence in the sense of "not presupposing each other's conclusions" holds, but in the sense of "not sharing common tools" is **insufficient**. Demonstration 3's asymptotic bound reuses Demonstration 1's formula, yielding **partial dependence**.

**Implication**: The "3 independent demonstrations" claim is closer to "3 variant demonstrations". Truly independent demonstrations require common-tool-free paths such as categorical / model-theoretic / combinatorial (Mk.IV task).

**Direct effect**: weakens the (a) side of the final (b-plus) statement.

---

### 5.2 Weakness 2 — BT-18 L5 BARRIER (Monster Moonshine <-> n=6 necessity not demonstrated) (SS 11.2)

**Issue**: In the Vacuum -> Monster 5-link chain, L5 (the n=6 coordinate necessity of 196884 = 196883 + 1 = Monster min faithful + trivial rep) is **not demonstrated (BARRIER)**. Borcherds 1992 Moonshine theorem guarantees that Fourier coefficients of $j(\tau)$ are sums of Monster representation dimensions, but there is no n=6 answer to **why Monster**.

**Implication**: the **largest unresolved barrier** of the Mk.III generation. If L5 is resolved, n=6 is confirmed as the organizational center of the whole of math; if it remains a permanent BARRIER, n=6 remains a "collection of beautiful numerical coincidences".

**Direct effect**: **only route** through which the final (b-plus) statement can transition to (a), and the **largest barrier**.

---

### 5.3 Weakness 3 — next-theorem 3-candidate DSE vs PAPER conflict (SS 11.3)

**Issue**: Among the three P6 candidates tau^2/sigma=4/3, sigma-tau=8, 1/n=1/6:
- **DSE verdict** (10-domain empirical average): A (tau^2/sigma=4/3) 10/10 PASS, ranks 1st

The two verdicts **directly conflict**. The cause: DSE uses average fit, PAPER uses maximum weight, and the PAPER weighting function gives bonuses to already-approved items — a **consistency bias toward existing promotions**.

**Implication**: **Next-theorem unique winner undecided**. The "theorem after sigma*phi=n*tau" is **not yet** determined in the Mk.III generation.

**Direct effect**: evidence that the Mk.III "completion" declaration is **partial**. The reason the P7-gamma closing paper (this one) leaves it as an **open question**.
