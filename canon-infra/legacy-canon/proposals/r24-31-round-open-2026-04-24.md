## Proposed R24+31 Topic

**Trilateral manifest coherence audit** — a bite-sized cross-axis verification round between axes A/C/D before committing to the much larger axis E.

### Statement

The three manifests (`invariants.json`, `silicon_functor.json`, `measurement_boundary.json`) were authored in three separate rounds (R24+26, R24+29, R24+30) and have never been *jointly* checked. R24+31 proposes a structural read-through that verifies:

1. **Disjointness**: no atom name appears in two manifests with conflicting classification (e.g. an atom declared in F's image must not be simultaneously declared in `measurement_boundary` as excluded from F).
2. **Crossover consistency**: every F ∩ G atom listed in `silicon_functor.json` (currently `warp_size`, `carbon_atomic`) is consistently represented in `invariants.json`'s tier_coverage / atlas_ref or is explicitly noted as a cross-functor fixed point.
3. **Boundary consistency**: atoms flagged in `measurement_boundary.json` as "outside F" are not reachable from `invariants.json`'s semiring closure on the 10 atoms at declared tolerance — i.e. the "borderline" `alpha_coupling` case aligns with R24+17's lenient-mode taxonomy as claimed.
4. **Atlas-ref coverage**: every `atlas_ref` line number cited across the three manifests resolves inside `atlas/atlas.n6` (21800 lines, 5928 @R entries, 326 @P, 357 @C, 255 @L) to the tag-class claimed (e.g. a cited `@P foundation` row is actually `@P foundation`).

### Deliverable

A single new witness row appended to `state/atlas_convergence_witness.jsonl` with `round: "R24+31"` and `event: "trilateral_manifest_coherence_audit"`, carrying:

- a 4-field pass/fail grade (one per check above)
- the list of any discrepancies found (if any)
- any atlas_ref line numbers that failed resolution
- classification of the round as `cross_axis_verification` (meta-level) rather than new atom discovery

**Config is not modified in R24+31.** If the audit surfaces a conflict, R24+32+ would carry the fix; R24+31 is read-only verification. This keeps the round small and reversible.

### Why This Topic (vs alternatives)

Two alternatives were considered:

- **New atom candidate**: R24+25 already declared atom inventory closed at 10, and R24+27 reasserted it as likely exhaustive. Any new-atom round would re-open a closed question and has low expected yield under `atom_inventory_closure_declared`. Not chosen.
- **Axis E full @R audit**: The 5924-entry audit is the correct long-term move but is large. R24+22 already random-sampled 5 entries (80% hit). A full audit deserves its own multi-round mini-series, not a single round slot. Defer.

**Trilateral coherence** is chosen because:

- It touches all three config manifests in a single pass.
- It is strictly read-only (no enforcement, no new atom, no large-scale scan).
- It closes a real integrity gap (three manifests written independently across 4 rounds, never jointly audited).
- It generates a clean precondition for any future R24+32 enforcement gate (P45/P46 candidates reserved at R24+30) — you cannot cleanly enforce lenient-vs-strict measurement policy without first knowing the three manifests agree.
- The round fits inside one session tick — no new scanner, no new atom, no new adapter.
